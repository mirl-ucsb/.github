# Code of Conduct

## Who we are

MIRL, the Material / Image Research Lab, explores the power of images and objects in shaping histories, identities, and cultural narratives. We foster a space for research, dialogue, and reparative practices that honor Indigenous, diasporic, and underrepresented perspectives in art, art history, architecture, and visual culture. These projects are an extension of that space, and everyone who takes part in them shares responsibility for keeping it welcoming, respectful, and free of harassment.

## Our commitment

We are committed to a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

MIRL stands against all forms of oppression, including white supremacy, settler colonialism, racism, xenophobia, classism, ableism, religious discrimination, transphobia, and homophobia. We ask everyone who participates to be mindful of their positionality and to engage with humility, accountability, and openness.

## Knowledge shared with care

Much of our work touches archives, objects, and images that carry histories of resilience, displacement, erasure, and reclamation. Treat what is shared here with care and respect, and honor the right of communities to represent their own visual histories on their own terms. Cultural expression takes many forms, from software to artistic practice to storytelling, and we welcome contributions that reflect diverse lived experiences.

## Expected behavior

- Be considerate and respectful, and assume good faith.
- Welcome newcomers, and make room for differing viewpoints and levels of experience.
- Give and accept feedback graciously.
- Remember that our communities span many disciplines, from art history to software, and that not everyone shares the same technical vocabulary.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination in any form.
- Demeaning, insulting, or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks.
- Publishing other people's private information without their explicit consent.
- Sustained disruption of discussion, or any conduct that would be inappropriate in a professional or academic setting.

## Calling in, not calling out

Mistakes are part of how we learn. When something goes wrong, we favor calling in over calling out: an invitation to reflect, to reconsider, and to grow together, rather than public shaming. We extend that good faith to one another while staying clear that the behaviors above are not acceptable.

## Reporting and enforcement

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please contact the MIRL team at mirl@arthistory.ucsb.edu, or Dr. Jeff O'Brien at jeffobrien@ucsb.edu. Reports will be handled discreetly.

In keeping with our ethic of calling in, maintainers will ordinarily begin with a private, constructive conversation. Where behavior is serious, repeated, or unsafe, maintainers may edit or remove contributions, restrict participation, or refer the matter to the appropriate offices at UC Santa Barbara.

## In closing

This code reflects our shared commitment to ethical, decolonial, and community-driven scholarship, and the values of the UC Santa Barbara community and the broader open-source and digital-humanities communities of practice.
