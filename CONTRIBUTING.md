# Contributing to MIRL projects

MIRL is the Material / Image Research Lab in the Department of the History of Art & Architecture at UC Santa Barbara. We build open tools for the arts, humanities, and cultural heritage, and our work is rooted in collaborative research and ethical engagement. We welcome contributions from researchers, students, curators, archivists, artists, and developers alike. You do not need to be a programmer to help, and we recognize that cultural expression takes many forms, from software to artistic practice to storytelling.

## Ways to contribute

- **Report a problem or request a feature.** Open an issue on the relevant repository, describing what you expected and what actually happened. Screenshots and small sample files help a great deal.
- **Improve the documentation.** Many of our users are not coders, so clear, plain-language documentation is as valuable to us as code.
- **Contribute code or content.** Fork the repository, create a branch, make your change, and open a pull request that explains what it does and why.

We welcome contributions that reflect diverse lived experiences, and we ask everyone to engage with the care, respect, and humility described in our [Code of Conduct](CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md).

## Using our templates

Several repositories (for example [mirl-map](https://github.com/mirl-ucsb/mirl-map)) are GitHub templates meant to be copied and adapted for your own project. If you are starting something new, use the green **Use this template** button rather than forking. Reserve pull requests for improvements to the tool itself.

## Working with us

For larger ideas, collaborations, or classroom use, please write to mirl@arthistory.ucsb.edu before investing significant effort, so we can point you in the right direction.

## Conduct and license

By taking part you agree to our [Code of Conduct](CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md). Unless a repository states otherwise, contributions are accepted under that repository's license, which for most of our projects is MIT.

## Working with cultural materials and sensitive data

Much of our work touches archives, objects, and images that carry living histories, including Indigenous, diasporic, and underrepresented perspectives. Please honor the right of communities to represent their own visual histories on their own terms, and do not contribute cultural materials, personal information, or identifying details without the consent of the people and communities involved. Some projects, such as [rescue-archiving](https://github.com/mirl-ucsb/rescue-archiving), also deal with at-risk media and the safety of those who provide it, so please keep real sensitive data out of issues, pull requests, and sample files.
